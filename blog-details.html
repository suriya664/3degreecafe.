Pour Over Rituals: Our Brew Ratios & Timing

Written by

Marcus Lee, Culinary Director

If you’ve ever sat at our brew bar watching a barista pour concentric circles over a V60, you’ve probably noticed how meditative the process is. Today we’re sharing our standard recipe—so you can recreate the ritual at home.

Our Equipment Checklist

  • V60 dripper & filters
  • Burr grinder set to medium-fine
  • Gooseneck kettle with temperature control
  • Scale with timer
  • Freshly roasted beans

Recipe Breakdown

We start with a 1:16 ratio—25 grams of coffee to 400 grams of water at 95°C. Rinse the filter, bloom with 60 grams for 45 seconds, then pour in three controlled pulses. Aim for a total brew time of 3:15.

“Consistency is born from intention—measure, time, taste, repeat.”

Experiment with agitation and grind size to highlight different flavor notes. Our current Ethiopian single origin shines with a slightly finer grind to bring out jasmine and stone fruit.

Pairing Suggestions

Pair your pour over with our seasonal olive oil loaf or a citrus scone to complement the coffee’s acidity. Let us know what you discover—tag us with #CafeWave.
